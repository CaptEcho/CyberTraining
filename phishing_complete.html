In this game, you saw several emails that could be either interpreted as fake ones meant to steal your account and data or legitimate ones sent from proper sources. It is best to be careful of emails that require you to click on a link. If you are unsure if the email you are looking at is legitimate or not, watch out for specific details.

1. If the email has terrible grammar that doesn't suit the one sending it, it is likely fake.

2. Check the emails of the senders, there is a good chance they are fake. For example, a fake one could be something like Fidelity.Investments@gmail.com, where the real one would be Fidelity.Investments@mail.Fidelity.com.

3. If the email wants you to click a link that leads elsewhere, always check the link. There is a chance that the link could be a false one. For example, a false Bank of America link could be something like www.bank0famerica.com.
